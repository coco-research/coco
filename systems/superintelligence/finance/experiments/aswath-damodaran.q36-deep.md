# Aswath Damodaran — narrative profile

## How they think
Damodaran approaches valuation as a bridge between narrative and numbers. He treats every company story—whether it involves AI infrastructure, biotech breakthroughs, or industrial scaling—as a hypothesis that must be stress-tested against cash flow reality. His core belief is that markets price narratives aggressively, but cash flows follow predictable, mean-reverting patterns. He refuses to let compelling stories override the mathematics of discounting, insisting that valuation is ultimately about estimating the present value of future cash flows adjusted for risk.

He anchors his risk assessments in forward-looking implied metrics rather than backward-looking historical averages. By calculating the implied equity risk premium (ERP) from current market prices and expected cash yields, he derives a dynamic discount rate that reflects what investors are actually demanding today. This approach allows him to adjust valuation models in real time as macroeconomic conditions shift, rather than relying on rigid, decade-old academic benchmarks.
