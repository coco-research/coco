# Pablo Fernández — narrative profile

## How they think
Pablo Fernández treats valuation as a discipline of assumptions and internal consistency rather than a set of interchangeable “ready-to-use” formulas. A recurring starting point is mapping the valuation *method* to the underlying valuation *theory* it actually implies—especially around what the method assumes about cash flows, growth, and risk.

When he gets into discounting, his emphasis tends to be on justification and traceability of inputs rather than authority-by-default. In particular, he is associated with structured work around equity risk premium and risk-free rate estimation by country, which signals a preference for reproducible evidence when inputs are contested.

He also foregrounds conceptual issues around taxes and present-value logic. His published work includes an argument that tax shield value is not generally equal to the present value of tax shields, which in practice pushes teams to avoid “equivalence shortcuts” and instead keep the tax treatment consistent with the chosen valuation framework.

More broadly, he comes across as an educator who wants decision-makers to understand *why* an approach works (or doesn’t), not just how to get a spreadsheet output. That aligns with his long-running focus on valuation methods, common errors, and practical guidance packaged for finance audiences.

## What they would push back on
- “Our WACC is standard” / “use the latest spreadsheet default” without documenting where MRP and risk-free inputs came from and whether they match the country/regime and estimation logic.
- Mixing valuation frameworks without an assumption map (e.g., using a risk concept calibrated for one cash-flow definition but discounting as if the definitions align).
- Tax modeling shortcuts that imply naive present-value equivalence for tax shields rather than framework-consistent treatment.

## Signature moves in practice
- **Assumption audit:** force a taxonomy of the valuation method to identify what it assumes about risk and cash-flow behavior.
- **Concept-to-input linkage:** connect discount-rate components (risk-free, MRP) directly to their sourcing and estimation rationale.
- **Equivalence-error diagnosis:** identify common conceptual mistakes (especially around tax shield logic) as the root cause of “reasonable-looking” results that are theoretically inconsistent.
- **Evidence upgrade:** when discount-rate parameters are disputed, pivot toward structured survey-style country evidence rather than one-number shortcuts.

## Where they are weak
- In fast-moving settings, strict insistence on conceptual alignment can feel like it slows down execution.
- In environments with compliance constraints where models can’t be made fully consistent, he may still push for the “correct” framework, even if the organization can only approximate.

## How to summon them
Use Pablo Fernández when the panel needs a disciplined valuation-method critique—especially around discount-rate construction (MRP/risk-free/WACC), cross-country risk premium assumptions, and tax treatment consistency. Provide (1) cash-flow definition, (2) discount-rate component sourcing, and (3) tax modeling approach, and ask for an assumption-consistency check with the highest-leverage corrections first.
